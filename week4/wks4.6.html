1. What is the difference between Phong shading and Phong lightining?

Phong shading introduces interpolating normals across each polygon. After having the normal at each point, we make independent shading calculations. It is a model applied per fragment.

Phong lighting refers to the reflection model that works on the vertices and simulates the interaction of light with surfaces.

2. What is the difference between flat shading, Gouraud shading, and Phong shading? List pros and cons of each. Is Gouraud or Phong shading the best method for simulating highlights?

Flat Shading: One light calculation per polygon, meaning the color is computed once for each polygon and the entire polygon is drawn with the same color. Each rendered polygon has a single normal vector that is used for shading.
It is less expensive to compute compared to other methods so it is suitable for using in applications with performance constraints. As disadvantages, it gives low-polygon models a faceted look.
Phong Shading: Uses interpolation shading, meaning the color is computed once per vertex using the normal of the vertex. The normals are interpolated between vertices, and lighting is calculated at a per-pixel-fragment level.
The results are realistic and it is more likely to capture specular highlights but it is computationally expensive.
Gouraud Shading: Calculates the color at each vertex of a polygon using the its normal and then interpolates these colors across the surface. It uses the interpolated colors to fill the polygon.
It offes smoother appearance than flat shading but it is more coputationally expensive. On the other side, is less expensive than Phong shading.

Both Phong shading and Gouraud shading may offer specular highlights but the Phong shading is the best choice as it can produce high-quality realistic shading because of how it handles interpolaration of normal vectors across the polygon. Gouraud on the other hand, may produce artifacts on highlights.

3. What is the difference between a directional light and a point light?

Point light is located somewhere in space and sends light equally in all directions. Whereas directional light does not have a specific location, the coordinates are irrelevant as it can be placed anywhere in space. It emits light in a specific direction.
Moreover, the distance of point light affects its intensity - it is decreased with the square distance from the source. On the other hand, directional light is positioned infenitely far away so the distance is irrelevant.

4. Does the eye position influence the shading of an object in any way?

Generally, eye position does not influence the shading since it relies on vertex normals. Though the shading is also depending on the object-space coordinates so in that way eye position may affect it when we have effects depending on the viewer's position.

5. What is the effect of setting the specular term to (0,0,0)?

It removes the reflection of light in the surface.

6. What is the effect of increasing the shininess exponent?

Higher shininnes result in a smaller and more concentrated highlights.
